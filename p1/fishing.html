Fishing

Big Pine California is also known as a fishing town, being surrounded by creeks, rivers, man-made and natural lakes, this town is the ideal getaway from the city to enjoy some prime fishing prospects.

The Palisade Mountain range has seven lakes fishermen and fisherwomen can catch out of. Having species like:

  • black bullhead
  • black crappie
  • bluegill
  • brown bullhead
  • largemouth bass
  • rainbow trout
  • northern pike
  • rock bass
  • smallmouth bass
  • yellow perch
  • common carp
    •

    Fishing at the Palisade Mountain Lakes

    At just under a half an hour drive from Big Pine can get you to a prime fishing spot at one of the Palisade Mountain Lakes. These man-made lakes are stocked with fish bi-annually and are surrounded by lush green forests. Depending on the time of year you go, the weather can be warm. What you can expect to catch here are what was listed above, these same fish are stocked at these lakes and people are encouraged to go fishing there.

    Fishing near Big Pine

    Other lakes you can travel to and hire a fishing guide at is the seasonal Lake Crowley which is about an hour drive. There, you can find veteran fishing guides with their own boats and equipment for rent.

    One such guide is Andre Nersesian of Eastern Sierra Sportfishing. He is a licensed guide and will expertly guide first timers to catching their first fish or take experienced fishermen and fisherwomen into the massive 12 mile long, 5 mile wide Lake Crowley.
