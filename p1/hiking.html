Hiking and Camping out of Big Pine

Big Pine is next to dozens of hiking trails of varying difficulties and camp sites. Being a very popular hikers town, Big Pine trails are very active and well maintained by the community. These same trails have existed since before the 1930's, well before the LADWP installed the man-made lakes, however, many trails go around the lakes and rivers that were created due to this undertaking.

Common Trails

Some of the amazing trails that are within 10 miles of the town include the Big Pine Lakes Trail, which has a length of 15.2 miles, as well as the Palisade Glacier Trail which is 15.9 miles. These are both great challenges and are not recommended for unexperienced hikers to undertake, there have been deaths on these trails are getting lost is a very real possibility as well as getting attacked by a wild animal.

There are also trails for the family such as the Big Pine Creek Campground and Glacier Lodge Loop that would take under an hour to walk and are very pet and children friendly. Being a little under 1.5 miles, this is ideal to get some fresh air as well as taking in the view of the Owens Valley.

Camping Near Big Pine

Big Pine, other than camping out while out on a trail, also has designated camp grounds for people looking to escape technology for some time. There are campgrounds found as high at 7,700 ft. elevation to the base of the valley where natural hot springs exist. Keough's Hot Springs is one of these locations. It was established in 1919 and has been remodeled continuously through the decades. With natural hotsprings as well as 1 jacuuzi and 1 pool being filed with natural spring water weekly, Keough's Hot Spring is a great place to go camping at. They also have options as to how you want to stay here from camping, RV sites and Rustic Funished Tent Cabins.
